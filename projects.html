PARK-IN SPOT

Autonomous parking that picks the closest open spot

Our system connects your agenda to a smart parking network. When you head to the supermarket, for example, the robot selects the closest available parking spot near that building and drives there autonomously.

How It Works

The system uses a Python script with OpenCV and a webcam to monitor the parking area. It identifies which parking spots are currently free and calculates the closest available one based on the user‚Äôs agenda or planned destination.


Once a parking spot number is selected, the robot car automatically follows a black line on the floor using infrared (IR) sensors to navigate through the parking layout. When it reaches the assigned spot, it performs the final parking maneuver autonomously.


I handled the robot hardware, embedded programming, motor control, and navigation logic. My teammates built the vision system and the environment setup.

Robobot

A robot that shows emotions using movement and light

Built from a recycled beer can, the robot reacts to human interaction. It wanders around freely, but if someone gets too close to its back or blocks its sensors, it ‚Äúgets mad,‚Äù flashes red LEDs, and quickly escapes.

How It Works

A sonar distance sensor lets the robot avoid obstacles and explore its environment autonomously. When an object or person approaches from behind, or tries to ‚Äúbully‚Äù it by covering its sensors. The Arduino triggers a defensive emotional reaction: faster movement, red warning LEDs, and escape behavior.


The goal of the project was to express personality and emotion through motion and light instead of facial expressions, making the robot feel alive.


I handled the entire build: mechanical design, character concept, Arduino programming, motor control, and behavior logic.
